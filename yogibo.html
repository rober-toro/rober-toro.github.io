Challenge

Global Brand, Regional Digital Ownership

Yogibo is a globally recognised brand with independently operating regional entities across Asia and beyond — each running its own localized strategy, each accountable for its own market. For Yogibo Singapore, that means owning every digital touchpoint for the Singapore and Malaysia market: the Shopify storefront, Lazada, and Shopee.

No digital decision moves without going through this role. The challenge is not just execution — it is making UI decisions that can be validated against live behavioral data and proven to move commercial outcomes, while keeping the brand visually coherent across every channel as the business grows.

Sustaining that standard as a one-person operation means building the asset library, documenting decisions as they ship, and ensuring nothing falls through the gap between design quality and business result.

Role Evolution

How the Role Grew

2023 - Shared contributor

Started as one of three designers supporting Yogibo Singapore through CTIA. Early work focused on content updates, visual assets, and page-level UI improvements.

2023-2024 - Increasing scope

Began taking ownership of deeper structural issues across navigation, mobile shopping flows, and performance. Also started coordinating more directly between external contributors and the Yogibo Singapore team.

2025 - Sole digital owner

Now owns the Shopify storefront end-to-end: UX decisions, theme customization, frontend implementation support, CMS updates, performance improvements, campaign visuals, and day-to-day digital maintenance. This period also included a major rebuild and update of the live storefront.

How Decisions Were Made

Without a formal research team, the decision loop relied on Microsoft Clarity and Google Analytics: heatmaps, session recordings, and behavioral patterns from the live store.

That meant using real user behavior to spot weak navigation paths, mobile PDP friction, and content gaps, then implementing changes directly inside the Shopify theme and watching how the store responded.

Takeaways

What This Work Demonstrates

Running the full digital stack of a live e-commerce store alone, without a formal handoff process, requires a different kind of design ownership. The work depends on spotting problems from live behavior, making decisions quickly, and living with those decisions in production.

Applied UX Judgment

Used Clarity and analytics to make practical decisions based on real shopping behavior instead of hypothetical flows.

Implementation Capability

Worked directly in Shopify Liquid, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript instead of stopping at design artifacts.

Commercial Ownership

Handled real business constraints, recurring updates, and production outcomes inside an active revenue-generating storefront.

Trust Built Over Time

Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Running a lean operation means decisions ship fast. What I'd do differently is build documentation into the cycle from the start — not as a portfolio afterthought, but as part of how decisions get made. That practice is now in place.
