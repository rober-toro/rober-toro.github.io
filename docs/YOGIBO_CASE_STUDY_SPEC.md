## 2. Positioning Summary

This case study is about what it looks like when a designer earns full digital ownership of a live commercial product over time. Starting as one of three contributors to Yogibo Singapore's digital work, Rober progressively took on more scope — UX, performance, mobile flows, CMS, content, and visual — until he became the sole person responsible for the entire Shopify stack. There is no Figma library handed off to a dev team here. The design is the code, implemented directly in a heavily customized Shopify theme Rober owns end-to-end. The case demonstrates applied UX judgment under real business constraints, without the safety net of a full team or formal process.

## 4. Recommended Case Narrative

**Main angle:** From shared contributor to full-stack digital owner — without a handoff in sight.

**Why this works for hiring managers:**

Most portfolio cases show a designer inside a process: research, wireframes, handoff, done. This case shows something rarer — a designer who owns the outcome, not just the deliverable. Rober used UX judgment (heatmaps, analytics, mobile audit), made decisions, and implemented them directly into a live commercial Shopify environment. No waiting for a developer. No Figma files sitting in review. When he identified that LCP was degrading performance, he fixed it. When mobile navigation was creating friction, he redesigned and coded it.

For hiring managers evaluating whether a candidate understands how digital products actually work in commercial environments — not just how to produce design artifacts — this case is the clearest evidence available.

It also shows trust accrual over time: going from one of three contributors to the single accountable person for a live revenue-generating store is not handed to someone who can't deliver.
